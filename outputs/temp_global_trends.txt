**TIME HORIZON**

### TREND: Crude Oil Price Surge and Global Stock Market Impact

**SUMMARY**
- Crude oil prices have surged, reaching over $100 per barrel, driven by the Iran war and supply disruptions.
- The surge in oil prices is affecting airlines, shipping, and other sectors reliant on diesel and fuel.

**BULLISH SECTORS**
- **Energy Sector**: ExxonMobil (XOM), Chevron (CVX).
- **Airlines with Hedge Strategies**: Delta Air Lines (DAL), American Airlines (AAL).

**BEARISH SECTORS**
- **Airlines**: Southwest Airlines (LUV), IndiGo (IDG).
- **Shipping and Logistics**: Maersk (MAK), CMA CGM (CGM).

**MAGNITUDE**
- **High**: The surge in oil prices is expected to have a significant impact on transportation and manufacturing costs, affecting stock prices in these sectors.

**TIME HORIZON**
- **Short (< 1 month)**: The immediate impact of oil price increases will be felt in the short term, affecting stock prices and economic indicators.

### TREND: Indian Rupee Depreciation and Foreign Portfolio Investors

**SUMMARY**
- The Indian rupee has depreciated to a historic low against the US dollar, driven by geopolitical tensions and rising crude prices.
- Foreign portfolio investors (FPIs) have withdrawn significant funds from Indian equities, reversing strong inflows from February.

**BULLISH SECTORS**
- **Rupee-Strong Sectors**: IT and pharmaceuticals, which are less sensitive to rupee depreciation.
- **Domestic Focused Companies**: HDFC Bank (HDB), Infosys (INFY).

**BEARISH SECTORS**
- **Export-Oriented Companies**: Tata Motors (TAMO), Hindustan Unilever (HUL).
- **Financial Services**: ICICI Bank (ICBK), Axis Bank (AXIS).

**MAGNITUDE**
- **High**: The rupee depreciation and FPI outflows are expected to have a significant impact on the Indian stock market and economic growth.

**TIME HORIZON**
- **Medium (1-6 months)**: The impact of rupee depreciation and FPI outflows will continue to influence the Indian stock market over the next few months.
